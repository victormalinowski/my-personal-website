About Me

Hi, I'm Victor! I am an undergraduate mathematics student at the University of Waterloo majoring in biostatistics. From an early age, I always enjoyed learning about math and science, which inspired me to seek opportunities to refine my abilities within these disciplines. In grade school I was particularly fond of partaking in school math competitions. Despite math being my primary area of study, I like exploring many other topics including history, geography, and astronomy.

Early in my undergrad career, I had the opportunity to acquaint myself with R Markdown during an introductory data analysis course. From there I began exploring other markup languages such as LaTeX and HTML, which led to the creation of this website. After deciding to major in biostatistics, I began studying stochastic processes, generalized linear models, time series analysis, experimental design, sampling theory, and life history analysis in greater detail.

From May 2022 to December 2022, I had the chance to work at Statistics Canada as a methodology co-op student. During my time at StatCan, I first used hierarchical linear models to complete a wellbeing study under the General Social Survey, after which I moved to the agriculture section to complete various innovation projects.
