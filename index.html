About Me

Hi, I'm Victor! I am a teaching assistant and PhD candidate in biostatistics at the University of Waterloo. My research interests include causal inference, longitudinal data, survival analysis, and life history analysis. From May 2022 to December 2022, I worked at Statistics Canada as a methodology co-op student, contributing to projects related to the general social survey and the census of agriculture. In June 2023, I graduated with distinction from the University of Waterloo with a Bachelor of Mathematics, majoring in biostatistics. I then earned a Master of Mathematics at the University of Waterloo, writing a research paper on causal inference under interference before graduating in October 2024.
